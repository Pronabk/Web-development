Sikkim

is notable for its biodiversity, including alpine and subtropical climates, as well as being a host to Kangchenjunga, the highest peak in India and third highest on Earth. Sikkim's capital and largest city is Gangtok.

Assam

is known for Assam tea and Assam silk. The state was the first site for oil drilling in Asia. Assam is home to the one-horned Indian rhinoceros.

Arunachal Pradesh

Popularly known as ‘Land of the Dawn-lit-Mountains’,the first Indian soil to greet the rising sun. Located on the northeastern tip of India with its borders touching China, Bhutan and Burma (Myanmar).

Tripura

Tripura is famous for its cultivations, tea plantations, beautiful gardens and much more. Being known for its breathtaking scenic view.

Nagaland

It is also popularly known as the 'land of festivals and every tribal festival is celebrated with pomp and gaiety, adorned with rich and colorful traditional attires. The most vibrant being the 'Hornbill Festival'.

Mizoram

"Mizo-ram" means "land of the Mizos" it's a state in Northeast India, with Aizawl as its seat of government and capital city

Manipur

is the birthplace of Raas Lila- a renowned form of classical dance which was created by Maharaja Bhagyachandra.

Meghalaya

also known as the Abode of Clouds, falls in one of the richest biodiversity areas in the world.

Northeast India

is the easternmost region of India. It comprises eight states Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, Tripura and Sikkim.
